Definition 
Managing General Agent is a role played by a wholesale insurance intermediary
with the authority to accept placements from (and often to appoint) retail
agents on behalf of an insurer. Managing general agents generally provide
underwriting and administrative services, such as policy issuance, on behalf of
the insurers they represent. These arrangements are most common in the surplus
lines marketplace. Typically, MGAs benefit insurers because such expertise is
not always available within the company and would be more costly to develop on
an in-house basis.
